Why do some people give up and others don't?

Every entrepreneur has failed. Even I have, even though I am very young (16) and barely an entrepreneur. I've struggled with over seven businesses that I gave up on. But why did I?

I failed six businesses. At my young age, this is a lot. But why did I? What made me give up, even though I watched a lot of motivational videos on Instagram which all conveyed the same message: "do not give up"

What was wrong with me back then? And what did I improve in my mindset to now be one hundred percent sure that I'll never give up on any business again?

I'll tell you honestly… If you give up on a project and don't start another one, you don't really want to succeed. And if you give up on a project and start another, it only delays your success.

I'll share my personal experience for both situations.

   My first Twitter accounts and the businesses affiliated with them were a total failure. Not because of the content, but because my vision for these projects was awful. I truly thought it was a total waste of time. Why? My account, like almost every account that has ever existed on X, was stagnating.

I thought this was a sign that my content was not worth continuing. So I gave up miserably and let other people succeed while I was wasting my life on random things. If I had kept working on my first account, I would have by far passed 20k followers on X (this was 3–4 years ago).

My mistake was concluding that my project would never gain followers because of stagnation. Back then, I didn't realize that every successful entrepreneur has experienced stagnation—often much worse than mine. For example, MrBeast waited four years before getting his first 1,000 followers on YouTube, with more than 250 videos published.

He was 13 and never gave up. Look where he is now. I don't need to tell you how successful he has become.

   My last Twitter account was given up because of a technical issue. I couldn't manage to reactivate it, and without waiting, I rushed to create another account. I'm confident that I'll succeed, but I lost a lot of time by abandoning the first one. It's not a huge deal, but it could have been avoided by being more serious and preventing all sorts of issues that make you lose time or money.

In conclusion, failing is what makes you learn. But what truly brings learning and success is not giving up. Be serious and disciplined. Even though you may think your project is a waste of time, either you convince yourself that this is how every business starts, or you immediately start another business after giving up. And don't forget: the important thing is not the destination, but the journey itself.
